A good girls guide to murder

"A Good Girl's Guide to Murder" by Holly Jackson is a thrilling young adult novel that follows the story of a high school student, Pip Fitz-Amobi, who sets out to solve a murder case in her small town. The book begins with Pip's senior project, where she chooses to investigate the five-year-old murder case of Andie Bell, a former student of her school. Andie's boyfriend, Sal Singh, was accused of her murder and then committed suicide, but Pip believes that there's more to the story than what the police reports say. As Pip delves deeper into the case, she uncovers shocking secrets and faces dangerous consequences. Holly Jackson has crafted a well-plotted and engaging story that keeps the readers on their toes. The characters are well-developed, and Pip, in particular, is a strong and relatable protagonist who is determined to find the truth, no matter the cost. The author also explores themes of friendship, family, and trust, which add depth to the story. One of the unique aspects of the book is its format. The story is told through Pip's senior project, which includes interviews, journal entries, and other documents. This adds a layer of authenticity to the story and makes it feel like a real-life investigation. Overall, "A Good Girl's Guide to Murder" is a compelling and suspenseful thriller that will keep readers engaged until the very end. It is an excellent choice for fans of YA mystery novels and anyone who enjoys a good whodunit. The book also has a satisfying conclusion that ties up all the loose ends, leaving readers feeling fulfilled.
